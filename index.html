Frequently Asked Questions

What is the purpose of the WHAC19 app?

The WHAC19 app is a tool to safeguard the welfare of Western Health doctors during COVID-19 and provide instant access to the most up-to-date information for time-critical procedures to ensure optimal patient care. The app contains information for frontline medical staff on subjects such as:

  • Putting on and taking off PPE (Personal Protective Equipment)
  • Taking care of your own welfare
  • Intubation and Extubation checklists and steps
  • Ventilation suggested initial actions and adjuncts
  • ICU General Care
  • ICU Tips your daily round and cross-skilling
  • Contact details and newsfeeds

Where does your medical advice come from? Are you credible?

The advice offered in the WHAC19 is offered by Staff Specialist in Anaesthetics Dr Gregg Miller, in direct consultation with the Western Health Department of Anaesthetics.

Who built the WHAC19 app?

WHAC19 is the brainchild of Dr Gregg Miller and was built rapidly with the help of more than 20 volunteer doctors, software developers and graphic designers. The Google Developer Group Melbourne For a full list of contributors, see the Contributors page.
